# CSAT Survey Findings

**Analysis Date:** February 5, 2026
**Data Sources:** Chat Channel, Email Channel, Enrollment Meetings
**Total Responses Analyzed:** 1,195
**Responses with Comments:** 686 (57.4%)

---

## Executive Summary

Overall customer satisfaction is **strong at 77.3%**, with Enrollment Meetings achieving the highest satisfaction (91.8%) and Email support showing the most room for improvement (61.7%). The data reveals that **human connection and responsiveness** are the biggest drivers of satisfaction, while **chatbot friction, unresponsiveness, and platform usability issues** are the primary sources of frustration.

**Key Insight:** Enrollment Meetings have dramatically higher satisfaction than async channels. Users value real-time, 1:1 human interaction, especially for complex enrollment decisions.

## Common Themes in Feedback

### Plan Selection & Comparison (73 comments)

Users frequently need help choosing between plans. When they get good guidance, it's a major satisfaction driver. When they don't, they feel lost.

**What works:**
> "It was easy to compare healthcare plans side by side."

> "Molly listened well, gave insight into my selection of plans, and based off what I had told her, confirmed the best decision for my family plan"

> "I was in a bind as I had selected a plan that was completely wrong for me. Benefits Bay was able to fix this within 36 hours and I'm back on track with my elections."

> "My customer service representative was wonderful to work with. She took the time to help me review the available insurance plans and find the best fit for me."

**What doesn't:**
> "What was my option and how do I keep my same health insurance"

> "I have been told that I have selected a healthcare plan but have yet to be able to login to the benefit bay system and do not know what this plan costs."

> "The counselor I had could not answer not one of my specific questions in regards to one of the plans I am considering and we didn't even make it to a second plan because she had another meeting."

### ICHRA Understanding (Limited but Critical)

ICHRA is a new concept for most users. When it's not explained well, users abandon the process entirely.

**What works:**
> "Kevin was a great help, took his time and explained the plans and allowances."

> "Lauren was very helpful and took great care in helping choose a plan, but I'm still confused as to how my employer is going to help with their contributions. Lauren and I ran out of time before I could understand."

**What doesn't:**
> "I found this whole process of an ICHRA not intuitive and provided little information throughout the process only to find out I didn't qualify. This is concerning because if on-boarding is this difficult, what will a claim be like... In the end, I am choosing to not get medical coverage through my employer mainly because the ICHRA process is nowhere near a mature product. This shopping list approach while great in concept, leaves no confidence on what we can anticipate our level of coverage will be."

**Product Implication:** This user chose to forgo employer coverage entirely due to ICHRA confusion. Clear education about how ICHRA works, what the employer contribution means, and what to expect is critical.

## What Makes Users Unhappy

### 1. Chatbot Frustration (Critical Issue)

The AI/chatbot experience is generating significant negative sentiment. Users find it repetitive, unhelpful, and a barrier to human help.

**Representative quotes:**
> "AI bot asks the same questions multiple times after I've already sent what I need done."

> "Dayna is great. The robo-gatekeeper before her is annoying."

> "Stayed on for too short, also your UX for the chatbot is horrendous and difficult to use. I really prefer to talk to a real actual person with a phone call, not some random chatbot."

> "Horrible its not answering anything im asking"

> "Chat bot asked me the same question 3 times."

> "I was directed to a live chat which was obviously NOT with a real person, then directed to schedule an appointment with a representative. You should of just directed me there in the first place."

**Product Implication:** The chatbot is creating friction rather than resolving issues. Consider clearer escalation paths to humans, reducing repetitive questions, and setting better expectations about bot vs. human availability.

### 2. Unresponsiveness and Ghosting

Users are being left without responses across all channels, leading to frustration and distrust.

**Representative quotes:**
> "I have tried multiple times to get in contact with someone, no one has emailed me or called back."

> "NO HELP, I'VE CHATTED, CALLED AND EMAILED."

> "I was corresponding with a BenefitBay support team member (Hannah Winters), and then she just stopped emailing me, pretty much leaving me to fend for myself."

> "Scheduled calls never happened. Tech chat not helpful. Phoned for support left message no one responded with a return call."

> "no one was available on Saturday to help me"

> "I never connected with a representative. I sent an email, and it was never responded to."

**Product Implication:** Need better queue management, SLA visibility, and fallback mechanisms when agents aren't available.

### 4. Provider Network Inaccuracy (Trust Issue)

Users are discovering that provider information shown during shopping is incorrect, leading to wrong plan selection.

**Representative quotes:**
> "I haven't had any negative issues with your employees. I am completely dissatisfied because when I signed up for insurance this year, it said our PCP was in network and it was wrong!"

> "I recently signed up for health coverage on their site. I checked in their site to see that all my providers were in network. After confirming this, I selected the appropriate plan. > "little to no guidance on how my PCP wasn't in network on the Avera plan when she works for Avera healthcare."

**Product Implication:** This is a major trust issue. Incorrect provider data leads to users selecting the wrong plan and feeling deceived. Provider directory accuracy should be a priority.

### 5. Enrollment Window and Timing Issues

Users feel rushed by short enrollment windows and encounter deadline-related stress.

**Representative quotes:**
> "The enrollment window was extremely short, and the process itself was difficult to navigate. I spent approximately three hours on the phone with Blue Cross Blue Shield, being transferred multiple times."

> "Missed open enrollment by a day. Now I have no insurance all year. Terrible"

> "Was not allowed to reshop; benefits close today."

> "Well I missed open enrollment since no one got back to me in time to talk me through it to understand more"

> "Open enrollment that starts during a holiday week, less than optimal."

**Product Implication:** Consider more proactive deadline reminders, longer windows where possible, and better communication about timing.

### 6. Preference for Human Contact

Many users explicitly state they want phone calls, not chat or email.

**Representative quotes:**
> "I would greatly appreciate an in person meeting or at the very least an actual person on a phone call."

> "I am unable to log into benefit bay and no one will help me. I have called and emailed several times. I need to speak to an actual human being."

> "I need to speak with a person not a robot on text"

> "To start, no one ever answers the phone. The only customer service ever rendered is an email reply. No one is ever able to pick up a phone and help resolve the issue at hand. Never."

**Product Implication:** While digital-first is efficient, high-stakes moments (enrollment decisions, complex issues) need clear phone/video options.

## Themes Summary

### What Delights Users
1. **Fast, accurate human responses**
2. **Patient, knowledgeable agents who explain things clearly**
3. **Proactive communication and follow-through**
4. **Feeling reassured during a stressful process**
5. **Easy-to-use chat when it connects to a real person**

### What Frustrates Users
1. **Chatbot that doesn't understand or help**
2. **No response / being ghosted**
3. **Platform bugs and login issues**
4. **Incorrect provider network information**
5. **Missing enrollment deadlines due to lack of support**
6. **No phone option when they need it**

---

## Product Recommendations

### High Priority

| Issue | Recommendation |
|-------|----------------|
| Chatbot friction | Reduce repetitive questions; clearer escalation to human; set expectations on bot limitations |
| Provider data accuracy | Audit provider directory data; add disclaimers; consider real-time carrier verification |
| Response SLAs | Implement queue management; surface wait times to users; ensure no ticket goes unanswered |
| Platform stability | Address login/auth issues; ensure consistent UX across sessions |

### Medium Priority

| Issue | Recommendation |
|-------|----------------|
| Phone access | Add clear phone support option for complex cases or escalation |
| Enrollment timing | More proactive deadline reminders; grace period handling for edge cases |
| Agent recognition | Celebrate top performers; share what makes them effective across team |

### Opportunities

| Opportunity | Why It Matters |
|-------------|----------------|
| Post-enrollment follow-up | Users feel reassured when agents follow up; builds trust |
| Proactive outreach | Users who miss deadlines often cite lack of contact; outbound calls could help |
| Self-service improvements | Many questions are simple (ID cards, payment status); better self-serve reduces ticket volume |
