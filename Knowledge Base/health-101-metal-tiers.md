# Metal Tiers

## Overview

Health insurance metal tier classifications in the United States categorize plans as Bronze, Silver, Gold, and Platinum based on the balance between premiums and out-of-pocket expenses.

## Bronze Plan

- Lowest monthly premiums
- Highest out-of-pocket costs
- Covers approximately 60% of healthcare expenses
- Suitable for generally healthy individuals prioritizing lower monthly payments

## Silver Plan

- Moderate premiums and out-of-pocket costs
- Covers about 70% of expenses
- Uniquely qualifies for cost-sharing reductions based on income eligibility
- Appeals to those seeking balance between premium and care costs

## Gold Plan

- Higher premiums but lower out-of-pocket costs
- Covers roughly 80% of healthcare expenses
- Targets individuals anticipating regular medical needs who prefer predictable costs

## Platinum Plan

- Highest-premium option
- Covers approximately 90% of expenses with minimal out-of-pocket costs
- Suits those expecting frequent medical care or seeking to minimize personal healthcare spending

Each tier represents different trade-offs between monthly affordability and actual care usage costs.
