# Bronze, Silver, Gold, and Platinum Metal Tiers

## Overview

ICHRA medical plans are categorized into four metal tiers that determine how costs are shared between you and your insurance carrier. The metal designation reflects cost-sharing percentages, not care quality.

## Key Differences

### Bronze Plans
- Lowest monthly premiums
- Insurer covers ~60% of medical costs
- High deductibles; often qualify as HDHPs
- Best for: Healthy individuals with minimal healthcare needs

### Silver Plans
- Moderate premiums and deductibles
- Insurer covers ~70% of medical costs
- Do not qualify as HDHPs
- Best for: Those wanting lower out-of-pocket costs than Bronze

### Gold Plans
- Higher premiums than Silver
- Insurer covers ~80% of medical costs
- Lower deductibles than Silver
- Best for: Those anticipating significant medical expenses

### Platinum Plans
- Highest monthly premiums
- Insurer covers ~90% of medical costs
- Lowest out-of-pocket costs
- Best for: Frequent healthcare users and those on multiple medications
